Cottonwood Canyons Foundation Site Redesign Project

CASE STUDY SUMMARY

The Design Team: TrailGuards

Designers: Jessica Allred, Rylee Muir, Lily Yang

Tools Used: Miro, InVision, Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe XD

Timeline: December 4-18, 2021

Introduction

The Cottonwood Canyons Foundation (CCF) is a nonprofit organization located in Salt Lake City, Utah. They have a mission to support the environment of the Cottonwood Canyons through stewardship and education.

This organization is kind of new and they have a strong mission statementand their site was created in 2020. Here is the link of their site: https://cottonwoodcanyons.org.

UX hypothesis

We believe that a responsive redesign for the Cottonwood Canyons Foundation will help to emphasize their focus on stewardship and education. By creating a clean, easy to navigate interface users will have a better experience when navigating the site and find the information quicker.

Research

User survey and usability test are conducted to get a better idea of how users are interacting with the key information on the CCF website.

Here are the findings from the user survey:

  • Not many people know about this wonderful organization
  • Users are likely to get involved in outdoor activism with the CCF through specific events
  • Users are more willing to be involved with free online educations

Research

Here are the feedback from the usability test:

  • Webpage is cluttered
  • Hard to skim the text
  • Lots of social media links

The user research result helps us to come up the UX hypothesis, the user insight, the problem statement, and the user persona - Lewis Johnson. The journey of Lewis helps us understand the user’s gain and paint.

Development - Branding

Through Lewis’s eye, we re-define the possible branding for CCF site with these keywords: inviting, adventurous, and encouraging.

Development - UI Architecture

UBased on site annotation and card sorting result we design the new navigation paths for Lewis. For the visual inspiration, we built mood board which provides us ideas of design styles, colors, fonts, button style, etc. We put all of these ideas into the style guide to make sure we three designers will follow this guide to do our work to ensure the consistency design of the site.

Wireframing, Testing & Prototyping

We start our wireframing from a rough drawing with mobile-first approach. Then we create a clickable low-fidelity prototype for the mobile version. We create a usability test plan based on the flow Lewis would browse the site with 3 tasks. We test with 7 users and the feedback is positive and all users can complete the tasks. We make some modifications before we build our hi-fi prototype: we put the special events on the landing pages, and we put drop-down menus in alphabetic orders.

Conclusion & Future Opportunity

We hope our redesigned website will help CCF get more recognized and supported. With more time, we would like to build more user flows and conduct more testing. We would like to get more involved with the state-holder -Joanna Wheelton when she comes back from her vacation. Building a better keyword-search list will help for users to find the CCF.
